A Little About Me

I grew up in New Delhi, studied English literature and psychology, and moved to Boston to pursue my Master's at Northeastern — where I discovered that my favorite thing in the world is making complex ideas click for people.

Whether it's teaching Ghalib's poetry to students who've never heard Urdu, building training frameworks for AI evaluators at Amazon, or writing about social innovators at MIT — the thread is always the same: I take something complicated and design a way for people to actually learn it.

That's what drew me to Learning Experience Design. It sits right at the intersection of everything I love — storytelling, structure, empathy, and problem-solving. I'm currently looking for full-time LXD roles where I can design learning experiences that make a real difference for learners.

My Design Process

How I approach every learning design challenge.

Discover

Stakeholder interviews, learner analysis, content audits, and gap identification

Design

Learning objectives (Bloom's), lesson flow (Gagné's Events), storyboards, and prototypes

Develop

Build in Articulate Storyline/Rise, Adobe Suite, with iterative review cycles

Implement

Pilot with learners, gather feedback, train facilitators, and launch

Evaluate

Measure impact using the Kirkpatrick Model, iterate based on data
