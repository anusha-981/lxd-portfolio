A Little About Me

I grew up in New Delhi, fell in love with English literature and psychology, and eventually found my way to Boston for my Master's at Northeastern, where I discovered what might be my favorite thing in the world: the moment a complicated idea finally clicks for someone.

That thread has run through everything I've done. Designing evaluation tools for AI annotators at Amazon. Building information architectures that turn complex data into clear decisions. Creating content systems at MIT that served three distinct audiences simultaneously. Each time, the work was really the same — take something layered and intricate, and design a way for people to actually navigate it, not just endure it.

Product Design felt like finding the name for something I'd already been doing. It lives right at the intersection of the things I care most about: user research, information architecture, interaction design, and the quiet satisfaction of a well-designed experience. I'm currently looking for roles where I can bring that same care to the people who use the tools I build.
